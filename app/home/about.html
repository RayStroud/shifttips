What is Shift Tips?

Shift Tips is a web app developed by Ray Stroud, a seasoned restaurant worker and a beginner web developer. It was made to keep track of restaurant job statistics, like hours, tips, sales, time spent waiting around for people to leave to be able to go home - the important stuff. It started as a spreadsheet, but it grew into an app in order to see weekly earnings and to figure out which days of the week were the best to work.

How to use Shift Tips?

After every shift, add whichever information is intended to be tracked (e.g. start/end times, tips, sales, covers, section, etc). It will immediately calculate some relevant information (e.g. hours worked, tip percentage, sales per hour, sales per cover, etc). As more shifts are entered, summaries can be viewed, like weekly and monthly statistics, or statistics by day of the week.

How to login to Shift Tips?

There are no passwords for Shift Tips accounts. The credentials are simply a Name and an associated unique Email address. To create an account, enter a Name and any valid and unique Email address into the New Account box, then click the Sign Up ( ) button. The email must not have been previously used for another account. To use an existing account, enter the Name and Email that were used during account creation into the Existing Account box, then click the Login ( ) button. To logout, visit the Account page and click the Logout ( ) button. If there are any concerns about accounts (e.g. the Name and/or associated Email have been forgotten), please Email the Administrator for help.

How to view/add/edit/delete a shift?

To add a shift, visit the Add Shift page. To view a shift that has already been added, visit the Shifts page and click on the desired shift to visit the Shift Details page. On the Shift Details page, the information can be modified by pressing either the Edit ( ) button or Delete ( ) button.

On the Add Shift or Edit Shift pages, enter or adjust whichever information is intended to be tracked, and click the Save ( ) button to submit the information. To disregard the new information, either navigate away from the page, or click the Cancel ( ) button on the Edit page to return to the Shift Details page.

Each shift requires a correctly formatted value in the Date field (YYYY-MM-DD). Every other field can be left blank, but each entered value must be formatted correctly. See "What do all the fields mean?" to determine how to correctly format each field.

What do all the fields mean?
Input Fields (Add / Edit Shift Pages)
Wage
(on Add / Edit Shift pages)		 This is the hourly wage, or what gets paid by the employer for each hour worked. It can be filled out every time a shift is added, or it can be set on the Account page, which will automatically fill it out on the Add Shift page.
Date This is the date of the shift. It is the only REQUIRED field to enter. It must be in the form YYYY-MM-DD, where YYYY is the four-digit year, MM is the two-digit month, and DD is the two-digit day (e.g. 2016-05-18). Note, dashes ( - ) must be used to separate the digits, not periods ( . ) or slashes ( / ). Some browsers have their own date-selector controls.
Start Time, Start This is the time the shift starts. It is used to calculate Hours. It must be in the form HH:MM and it must be in 24 hour format (e.g. 4:00 PM is 16:00). Some browsers have their own time-selector controls.
End Time, End This is the time the shift ends. It is used to calculate Hours. It must be in the form HH:MM and it must be in 24 hour format (e.g. 11:00 PM is 23:00). Some browsers have their own time-selector controls.
First Table This is the time when the first table was sat in the section. This is mostly irrelevant, so fill it out for curiousity's sake only. It might eventually lead to seeing "how many hours spent waiting for a table". It must be in the form HH:MM and it must be in 24 hour format (e.g. 4:00 PM is 16:00). Some browsers have their own time-selector controls.
Camp Hours, Camp This is the hours spent waiting for a table to leave before able to go home. It is recommended to track in 0.5 hour increments, although it is rounded to the nearest 0.01 hours.
Sales This is the sales during the shift. It is used to calculate %Tips, %T/O, Sales/h, Sales/cov. It is rounded to the nearest cent.
Covers, Cov This is the amount of covers, or the amount of people served during the shift. It is used to calculate Sales/cov. It must be a whole number with no decimals.
Tipout, T/O This is the amount of tips that are shared with other coworkers, usually a set percentage of sales, which is determined by the restaurant's policies. It is used to calculate %T/O. It is rounded to the nearest dollar.
Transfer, Tsfr This is the amount of tips that are shared with other coworkers as a result of transfers, which is when people move to a different server. It may or may not apply based on the restaurant's policies. It is rounded to the nearest dollar.
Cash This is the amount of cash that was earned after sharing tips (i.e. after tipout and/or transfers). It is rounded to the nearest dollar. It may be $0 if the restaurant has a "dueback policy", where tips received by card payments are not distributed until a later date.
Due, Dueback This is the amount of money to be retrieved later because of tips left by card payments. It may or may not apply based on the restaurant's policies. It is rounded to the nearest dollar.
Section This is the section assigned for the shift. It is recommended to use table numbers seperated by commas and no spaces (e.g. 1,2,3,4). There is a limit of 25 characters.
Cut This is a classification describing when a server was "cut", or stopped being seated for the night. It is a single letter or number, which is used as an abbreviation. Common terms: [S]tay, [G]o, [O]pen, [C]lose, [/] for split, etc.
Notes This is for any notes about the shift. It is used for recording any information that is not recorded in other fields. There is a limit of 1000 characters.
Calculated Fields
Hours This is the hours worked during the shift. It is calculated by the difference between the Start and End times.
Wage This is the total dollars that were earned due to the hourly wage. It is calculated using the Wage and Hours.
Tips This is the total dollars that were earned from tips. It is calculated using Cash and Due.
Earned This is the total dollars earned due to both the hourly wage and tips. It is calculated using Wage and Tips (which are calculated using Wage, Start, End, Cash, and Due).
%Tips This is the percentage of dollars earned in tips compared to sales. It is calculated using Sales and Tips (which is calculated using Cash and Due).
%T/O This is the percentage of dollars of tips shared with staff compared to sales. It is generally a fixed amount based on restaurant's policies. It is calculated using Sales and Tipout.
%TvW This is the percent of dollars earned by tips compared to wage, e.g. a %TvW of 200% means that twice as much was earned in tips compared to wage. It is calculated using Wage and Tips (which are calculated using Wage, Start, End, Cash, and Due).
Sales/h This is the sales per hour. It is calculated using Sales and Hours (which is calculated using Start and End).
Sales/cov This is the sales per cover, also known as "average guest check", which is how much each guest ordered that shift, on average. It is calculated using Sales and Covers.
$/h This is the total dollars earned per hour, including tips and wage. It is calculated using Tips, Wage, and Hours (which are calculated using Wage, Start, End, Cash, and Due)
L/D This is an abbreviation for Lunch and Dinner. Lunch shifts are any shift that starts before 2PM. Shifts are separated into lunch and dinner shifts because Sales and %Tips are usually much different between them.
Shifts
(on Summary, Period pages)		 This is how many shifts are in each summary group (e.g. there are 8 shifts in the "D Mon" summary group, which means that there are 8 Monday Dinner shifts.)
How to use the table functions?

header sort reverse sort

Each table has a header row with a dark-grey background and white text, which is the categories of the information that is displayed underneath. Any header that can be sorted will lighten when the cursor hovers over. Clicking any of these header names will change the background to blue and sort the information based on that category. Initially, there will be a black bar on the bottom of the header name, which indicates that the information is sorted alphabetically or numerically from lowest to highest. Clicking the blue sorted-header name will move the black bar to the top of the header name and flip the direction of the sorted information.

At the bottom of each table is a summary row with a medium-grey background and black and grey text. The averages of all the information in each category are in the top row in black text, and the totals are in the bottom row in grey text.

On the Shifts List page, hovering over a row will highlight the row in blue. Clicking on the row will navigate to the Shift Details page for that shift.

How to use the filters?

On the Shifts page, there is a choice of View Style, which displays shift information as either a list ( ) or as a weekly grid ( ). On the Summary and Period pages, there is a choice of Summary Type and Period Type, which changes how the information is grouped in the summaries.

To filter the information further (by date, day, and time), expand the Filters bar by pressing the View ( ) button. After selecting the filters, the menu can be collapsed by pressing the Hide ( ) button. The text size of the information can be adjusted by clicking the Text Size ( ) button. At any point, the filters can be reset by pressing the Reset ( ) button. On the Shifts Grid page, the weeks can be arranged either chronologically ( ) or reverse-chronologically ( ).

The Date Range filters limit shifts from a start period to an end period, e.g. selecting all the shifts of a certain month or year. The dates must be entered in a YYYY-MM-DD format, where YYYY is the four-digit year, MM is the two-digit month, and DD is the two-digit day (e.g. 2016-05-18). Note, dashes ( - ) must be used to separate the digits, not periods ( . ) or slashes ( / ).

The Lun/Din filter limits shifts to either Lunch ( L ), Dinner ( D ), or both. Lunch shifts are any shifts that start before 2PM. When a button is white, it is disabled, and clicking the button will enable it and change its color. When a button is colored, it is enabled, and clicking the button will disable it and change it to white. They are toggle buttons, which means only one can be selected at a time, so enabling one button will automatically disable the other button.

The Day of Week filters (only on the Shifts page) limit shifts to any combination of days of the week, e.g. Saturday (Sat) and Sunday (Sun). When a button is white, it is disabled, and clicking the button will enable it and change its color. When a button is colored, it is enabled, and clicking the button will disable it and change it to white. Any combination of buttons can be enabled or disabled.

How to use the view settings?

On the Account page, the View Settings affect what fields are displayed on this device and this browser only. If the device or browser memories are cleared, it will return to the default settings. Use the Save Current View Settings ( ) button to upload the current settings to be able to download them on other devices or browsers. Sometimes the settings will reset if the app's internal settings have been changed by the developer.

There are prearranged settings buttons in the View Settings Type area, including custom settings that were Saved by the user. In the Table Controls, use the Expand All ( ) and Collapse All ( ) buttons to easily navigate through the tables. The buttons will turn blue when they are activated, and they will turn white when deactivated. The text size of the tables can be adjusted by clicking the Text Size ( ) button.

The View Settings are arranged into different tables corresponding to pages within the app. Each table will determine which fields are displayed on the corresponding page. Clicking the table title will either collapse ( ) or expand ( ) the table to reveal the fields corresponding to that page. Clicking the button beside each field will toggle between the green Visible ( ) button and the red Hidden ( ) button.

The Set Wage area in the Add Shift Page table controls the default wage that is entered on the Add Shift page. To change the default Wage value, enter a decimal value in the white input box, then click the Save ( ) button to submit the value. The current default Wage value, if there is one, is displayed above the Save button. This is different than the Wage field and Visible button below which controls whether the Wage field is displayed on the Add Shift page.

How to keep track of duebacks?

When adding a shift with a non-zero amount in the Due field, it will assign the shift an amber Not Retrieved ( ) icon that shows that the dueback has to be retrieved. Duebacks can be managed shift-by-shift on the Shift Details pages, or they can be managed on the Duebacks page.

On the Duebacks page, it shows a list of all the Unretrieved duebacks. In the Filters bar, the list can be changed to show All Duebacks (retrieved or not), or show All Shifts (even those without duebacks). To mark a dueback as received, click the amber Not Retrieved ( ) button, and it will change to a green Received ( ) button. If the button was clicked by mistake, clicking it again will swap it between either amber Not Retrieved or green Received. The dueback status buttons may need to be clicked more than once to change their status. It is a known issue that has yet to be resolved.

There are two other possible values for the dueback status: Unknown ( ) is for when a dueback's status is not known or not remembered, and Lost ( ) is for when a dueback was not there when retrieved, and therefore not received. To assign or change a dueback status of Unknown or Lost, click the the blue View ( ) button to redirect to the Shift Details page, or navigate to the Shift Details page from the Shifts page. On the Shift Details page, there is a tiny button beside the Due value that shows the status of the dueback. Clicking the button will cycle between the four values.

How to use the summaries?

On the Summary page and the Period Summary page, there are tables that show calculated averages and totals based on a selection of shifts. Click a button in the Summary Type or Period Type section to change how the summary information is grouped. The Row Type section toggles what information is displayed in each row: averages ( AVG ), totals ( TOT ), or both. A row of averages will show the information for one average shift in that particular summary group (e.g. a Monday shift is on average 5 hours long, or an August 2016 shift has an average of $1150 in sales). A row of totals will show the information for all shifts in that particular summary group (e.g. Monday shift is on average 5 hours long, or an August 2016 shift has an average of $1150 in sales). When both buttons are white, both types of rows are displayed. Clicking a white buttons will change the button to blue and display only that type of row. Clicking a blue button will change the button to white and show both types of rows.
